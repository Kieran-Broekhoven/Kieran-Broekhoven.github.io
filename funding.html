Did You Know?

According to the department of Health and Human Services, emergency domestic violence shelters had to turn away 172,000 requests for help in FY 2010 due to lack of capacity and resources. Since 2007, the number of denied requests for help have steadily rose due to shelters' lack of resources and room availability.

On a given day, domestic violence shelters in the state of California serve about 5,500 victims of domestic violence. However, in the same period, over 1,000 requests for help go unmet due to lack of funding and resources. Source: National Network to End Domestic Violence
